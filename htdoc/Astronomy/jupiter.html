Jupiter

This is a series of images I took of Jupiter with my Celestron NexStar 8se by holding my iPhone up to the eye piece (25mm Plossl). (I am still working on getting the NexImage 5 to work on a iMac running VMWare). This is also through my star diagonal so it is still mirrored left-right; I did not filp it from what I saw when I took it. It looks a lot better to the eye and with out my glasses on. I can easily make out the stripes in the clouds of Jupiter, but not the red spot. I usedSolar Walk to work out the order of the moons, but something is not right.

This is my updated version of the Sidereus Nuncius Medicean drawings Galileo made when he was the first human to see there where moons orbiting other bodies in the solar system.

The orbital period of the moons are:

  • Io 1.8 days
  • Europa 3.5 days
  • Ganymede 7 days
  • Callisto 16.7 days
